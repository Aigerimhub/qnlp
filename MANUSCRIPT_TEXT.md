# Replacement Text for the Manuscript

## Dataset Paragraph

The study uses a dedicated corpus of publicly accessible policy and regulatory documents collected from official institutional websites and administrative repositories. The collection includes university regulations, scholarship policies, eligibility rules, administrative procedures, and compliance-related documents. The corpus was segmented into clause-level units and annotated with relevant evidence links and three decision labels: *Eligible*, *Not Eligible*, and *Needs Review*. A dedicated corpus was used because existing benchmarks such as LexGLUE and PROVBENCH focus primarily on legal language understanding or provision retrieval and do not directly represent multi-clause evidence assembly for policy-oriented eligibility and compliance decisions. The source manifest, corpus statistics, annotation guidelines, split information, dataset schema, and representative records are available in the public project repository.

## Data Availability

The source code and trained model artifacts are publicly available through the LexIR Hugging Face repository. Dataset documentation, including the source manifest, corpus statistics, annotation guidelines, split information, machine-readable schema, and representative records, is available through the QNLP GitHub repository. The original policy and regulatory documents remain accessible through their official public sources and are referenced by their source URLs rather than redistributed. The complete curated corpus is not included in the current public release unless explicitly stated in the repository.
